Based on common hospital IT needs outside the EHR:

  #: 1
  Application: Staff Credentialing & Privileging
  Why Hospitals Want It: Tracking physician licenses, board certs, DEA registrations, malpractice insurance, and privileges across departments. Accreditation bodies (Joint Commission) audit this constantly. Most hospitals run this on
    spreadsheets or legacy systems.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  #: 2
  Application: Clinical Equipment / Biomedical Asset Management
  Why Hospitals Want It: Tracking 10,000+ medical devices (ventilators, pumps, monitors) — maintenance schedules, FDA recall compliance, calibration, location tracking, and useful life. Different from IT asset management — governed by
    CMS and FDA.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  #: 3
  Application: Patient Safety / Incident Reporting (Event Management)
  Why Hospitals Want It: Near-miss and adverse event reporting, root cause analysis, CAPA tracking, and regulatory disclosure (state reporting, CMS). Needs anonymous submission, severity scoring, and trending dashboards for quality
    committees.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  #: 4
  Application: Compliance & Regulatory Readiness
  Why Hospitals Want It: Managing Joint Commission, CMS Conditions of Participation, state health department, and OSHA requirements — tracking survey findings, corrective actions, policy attestations, and mock survey results across
    hundreds of standards.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  #: 5
  Application: Vendor Credentialing & Access Management
  Why Hospitals Want It: Controlling which sales reps and service techs can enter the facility, which departments they can access, verifying their immunizations/background checks, and logging visits. Driven by infection control and OR
    security policies.

  The common thread: accreditation, safety, and regulatory compliance workflows that Epic doesn't cover. These are operational/administrative processes with heavy audit requirements where hospitals are still using paper, SharePoint, or
   disconnected point solutions.
